      Here is my -> second proposal


Computers are amazing information processors, but they are also cultural toys. Computer games and amusements still dominate mass-market sales. When we "play" with them, we obey "totally binding" logical norms of information processing. Moreover, as play, this rule-governed activity justifies itself. We do it because "it's there" and we can. Therein lies the impression of pleasure as the psychological partner of computer play: the captivation, tension, delight, and awareness of play's distinctiveness from "regular life"

More than psychology is involved. Play implies looseness. A loose nut in a machine plays; a loose nut disrupts social order; a poor pun plays with word meanings. Such looseness in today's information originates from its lack of closure in knowledge, the arbitrariness of symbols, and the union of digital symbol with electronic computer, whose high-speed, algorithmic, iterating capacities may develop fresh forms of information.

Profusion, complexity, and emergence are three aspects of play that characterize our period. Each of these concepts extracts a deeper and richer vein of intellectual and cultural Mplay from the computer's micro-informational capability. The use of raw computer power in digitizing macro-information resulted in these expansions. Play provides knowledge with power. Together, they have severed information from its centuries-old, intuitive meaning as the molded or informed kernel of content we derive from experience. Now, logical rules, technological speed, power, and play bracket our global exchanges.
